Software Requirements Specification (SRS)
Mental Health Care Patient Management System:

1. Introduction

The Mental Health Care Patient Management System is a software system that is designed to manage the records of patients undergoing mental health treatment. The system aims to improve patient care by providing a secure and efficient way of managing patient information, appointments, medication, and treatment plans.

1.1 Scope
The system will allow healthcare providers to manage patient information, appointments, and medication information. Patients will be able to access their own information, view their treatment plan, and schedule appointments.
1.2 Objectives
The objectives of the system are to:
Improve patient care by providing efficient and secure management of patient information. Enhance collaboration among healthcare providers to ensure better patient outcomes. Increase patient engagement by providing them access to their own information and the ability to schedule appointments.
1.3 Definitions, acronyms, and abbreviations
MHCPMS (Mental Health Care Patient Management System) EHR: Electronic Health Record

2. Functional Requirements

2.1 Patient Registration
The system shall allow healthcare providers to register new patients by collecting the following information: name, date of birth, address, phone number, email address, and insurance information.
2.2 Patient Information Management
The system shall allow healthcare providers to view and manage patient information, including demographics, medical history, diagnosis, treatment plan, and medication information.
2.3 Appointments
The system shall allow healthcare providers to schedule, modify, and cancel patient appointments. Patients will be able to view their appointments and request changes.
2.4 Medication Management
The system shall allow healthcare providers to manage patient medication information, including prescriptions, dosages, and side effects.
2.5 Treatment Plans
The system shall allow healthcare providers to create, view, and modify patient treatment plans.
2.6 Reporting
The system shall generate reports on patient demographics, appointment scheduling, medication management, and treatment plans.
2.7 User Access Management
The system shall allow healthcare providers and administrators to manage user accounts and access to patient information.

3. Non-Functional Requirements

3.1 Performance
The system shall be able to handle a large number of patient records and user requests with minimal latency.
3.2 Security
The system shall be secure and comply with HIPAA regulations to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of patient information.
3.3 Usability
The system shall be easy to use and navigate for healthcare providers and patients.
3.4 Reliability
The system shall have a high level of uptime and availability to ensure that healthcare providers and patients can access it when needed.

4. User Interface Requirements

4.1 Layout and Design
The system shall have a clean, modern, and intuitive user interface that is easy to navigate.
4.2 Accessibility
The system shall be accessible to users with disabilities and comply with WCAG 2.1 guidelines.

5. System Architecture

5.1 Technology Stack
The system shall be developed using the following technologies: Java, Spring Framework, MySQL, and HTML/CSS/JavaScript.
5.2 Deployment
The system shall be deployed on a cloud-based platform, such as Amazon Web Services or Microsoft Azure.

6. Data Model

6.1 Patient Data
The system shall store patient data, including demographics, medical history, diagnosis, treatment plan, and medication information.
6.2 User Data
The system shall store user data, including user roles, access permissions, and login information.

7. Assumptions and Dependencies

7.1 Data Import
The system assumes that patient data can be imported from existing electronic health record (EHR) systems.

8. Constraints

8.1 HIPAA Compliance
The system must comply with HIPAA regulations to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of patient information.
8.2 Budget
The system must be developed within the allocated budget of $500,000.
8.3 Timeframe
The system must be developed and deployed within 12 months of the start of the project.

9. Legal and Regulatory Requirements

9.1 HIPAA Compliance
The system must comply with HIPAA regulations to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of patient information.
9.2 Data Protection
The system must comply with applicable data protection laws, such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

10. Acceptance Criteria

The system will be considered acceptable if it meets the following criteria:
All functional requirements have been implemented and are working correctly. Non-functional requirements are met, including performance, security, usability, and reliability. The user interface is intuitive and easy to use. The system complies with HIPAA regulations and other legal and regulatory requirements. The system is deployed within the allocated budget and timeframe.

11. Glossary

EHR: Electronic Health Record HIPAA: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act WCAG: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
By following the above SRS document, a mental health care patient management system can be developed to provide efficient and secure management of patient information, enhance collaboration among healthcare providers, and increase patient engagement.
